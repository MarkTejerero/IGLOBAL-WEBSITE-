Other Australian Visas

Specialized visa options for unique circumstances

Specialized Visa Options

Australia offers various specialized visa categories for people with unique circumstances, specific skills, or special situations. These visas cater to diverse needs and provide pathways for different types of migration.

Work Visas

Temporary Skill Shortage (Subclass 482)

For skilled workers sponsored by Australian employers to fill genuine skill shortages.

Streams:
  • Short-term stream (2 years)
  • Medium-term stream (4 years)
  • Labour Agreement stream

Employer Nomination Scheme (Subclass 186)

Permanent visa for skilled workers nominated by their Australian employer.

Streams:
  • Direct Entry stream
  • Temporary Residence Transition
  • Agreement stream

Regional Sponsored Migration (Subclass 187)

Permanent visa for skilled workers nominated by employers in regional Australia.

Note:
  • Closed to new applications
  • Replaced by Subclass 494
  • Processing existing applications

Working Holiday Visas

Working Holiday (Subclass 417)

For young people aged 18-30 from eligible countries to holiday and work in Australia.

Features:
  • 12 months initial stay
  • Work up to 6 months per employer
  • Study up to 4 months
  • Multiple entry visa

Work and Holiday (Subclass 462)

For young people aged 18-30/35 from specific countries with higher education requirements.

Requirements:
  • Tertiary education or trade qualifications
  • 12 months stay
  • Work and study opportunities
  • English language requirements

Post-Study Work Visas

Temporary Graduate (Subclass 485)

For recent graduates from Australian educational institutions.

Streams:
  • Graduate Work: 18 months for specific qualifications
  • Post-Study Work: 2-4 years based on qualification level

Visa Extension Opportunities

Additional time for graduates in certain circumstances.

Extensions available for:
  • Regional study graduates
  • Certain qualification levels
  • Specific geographic areas
  • Skills shortage occupations

Special Category Visas

Special Category (Subclass 444)

For New Zealand citizens to enter and remain in Australia.

Features:
  • Automatic grant on arrival
  • Live, work, and study rights
  • No application required
  • Valid passport needed

Protection Visas

For people seeking Australia's protection as refugees or asylum seekers.

Types:
  • Temporary Protection (785)
  • Safe Haven Enterprise (790)
  • Protection (866)

Medical Treatment (Subclass 602)

For people who need to enter Australia for medical treatment or as a medical escort.

Purposes:
  • Medical consultation
  • Medical treatment
  • Organ donation
  • Medical escort duties

Regional Australia Visas

Skilled Work Regional (Subclass 491)

Points-based visa for skilled workers nominated by state/territory or sponsored by eligible relatives.

Pathways:
  • State/Territory nominated
  • Family sponsored
  • Live and work in regional Australia
  • Pathway to permanent residence

Skilled Employer Sponsored (Subclass 494)

For skilled workers nominated by approved employers in regional Australia.

Benefits:
  • 5-year visa validity
  • Regional work requirement
  • Employer nomination required
  • Pathway to permanent residence

Important Considerations

Visa Conditions

Each visa has specific conditions that must be complied with. Failure to comply can result in visa cancellation.

Processing Times

Processing times vary by visa type and individual circumstances. Apply well in advance of intended travel.

Professional Advice

Immigration law is complex. Seek professional advice to ensure you choose the right visa pathway.
